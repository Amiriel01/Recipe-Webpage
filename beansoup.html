Bean Soup

Pictured: A hearty dinner choice that is even better the next day!

Are you wondering what to make on a busy weeknight? This recipe is a great choice for cold winter nights when you need a hearty comfort pick recipe.

This recipe is a great one to make ahead and eat the next day. The flavors have time to merge when made a day in advance. This recipe holds well in the refridgerator for great leftovers too!

Ingredients

  • 6 15 oz. cans Great Northern Beans
  • 4 c. Chicken Broth
  • 1 tbsp. Italian Seasoning
  • 12 oz. Bacon, Cooked and Crumbled
  • 1 tsp. Garlic Powder
  • 1 tsp. Onion Powder
  • 1 tbsp. Butter
  • 1/3 lb. Carrots, Shredded (Optional)

Steps

  1. Place butter in a large pot to melt.
  2. Open and pour cans of beans in the pot.
  3. Add the chicken broth to the pot.
  4. Add the garlic powder, onion powder, and italian seasoning to the pot.
  5. Crumble the cooked bacon and add it to the soup.
  6. Add shredded carrots to the soup pot.
  7. Stir all the ingredients and set the heat to medium.
  8. Cover the pot and simmer for one hour, stirring occasionally.
  9. Remove the lid and mash the beans. I use the bottom of a coffee mug, you can also use a sturdy spoon.
  10. Replace the lid and simmer another hour.
  11. Remove the soup from the burner and let cool. Eat today or enjoy it tomorrow!
